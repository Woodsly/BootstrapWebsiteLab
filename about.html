I was born and raised in Grosse Pointe, MI. Growing up, I was pretty much always on the computer if I wasn't biking around the town with my friends. I found a game called Runescape when I was 6 years old and instantly fell in love with it - I actually am still playing today. Besides gaming, I also enjoyed playing soccer and baseball. I ended up choosing soccer over baseball, and played through my sophomore year of highschool. The following year I made the decision to switch to cross country and ran for my last 2 years of highschool. I also played the saxophone and was in the school band for all of my years.

Growing up, I always knew I was going to go to Michigan State for college. Following highschool I was accepted into Michigan State's Human Biology program. I didn't know what kind of job I wanted after school, all I knew was that I loved biology. After talking with the guidance counselors I eventually found pharmacy, and that became my main focus. Following graduation I wanted to wait before applying to pharmacy school so I could get some experience in the field. Pharmacy school is a very big commitment and isn't cheap, so I wanted to make sure it was what I really wanted to do. I eventually got a job as a pharmacy technician at RiteAid and had my eyes opened to how terrible pharmacy really is. Working in practically any pharmacy is the equivalent at working at McDonald's, except when you tell someone their happy meal is $5, they get angry and say it should be free through their insurance, even though they're currently uninsured. One of the pharmacists I worked with said I should do something with computers. That was all the convincing I needed to get out of pharmacy and look for other options which eventually lead me here, to Grand Circus.
